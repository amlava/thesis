\chapter*{Abstract}

We study stochastic convex optimization problems with almost sure affine
inequality constraints of the form
\begin{equation*}
    \begin{aligned}
    &\min_{x \in \mathbb{R}^n} \, \{\, f(x) := \E(F_\xi(x)) + r(x) \,\} \\
    &\textup{subject to} \quad A(\xi) x - b(\xi) \leq 0 \quad \textnormal{almost surely},
    \end{aligned}
\end{equation*}
allowing for infinitely many constraints and composite objectives.
To handle the constraints without projections, we develop a single-loop
penalty-based method inspired by smoothed exact penalties, combined
with proximal operators to accommodate nonsmooth regularizers and more
general objective structures. Under reasonable assumptions, we prove convergence in both
the convex and strongly convex settings.
In particular, our method attains rates of order
\[
    \mathcal{O}\Bigl(\frac{\log^{1/2+\epsilon} K}{\sqrt{K}}\Bigr)
    \quad\text{and}\quad
    \mathcal{O}\Bigl(\frac{\log^\epsilon K}{K}\Bigr),
\]
for any $\epsilon>0$, respectively, matching the optimal stochastic rates
up to arbitrarily small logarithmic factors, while remaining projection-free.
Unlike several related methods, our analysis does not require a global bound
on the variance of stochastic gradients, and allows for infinitely many constraints.
Numerical experiments support the theoretical
findings and illustrate the practical performance of the method.
